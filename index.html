about me

Prior to pursuing a Computer Science Degree at Georgia Tech, I gained a curiosity for technology and programming from my initial exposure to online games as a child. Early online community games such as Millsberry, Runescape, and Club Penguin, to name a few, gaged my interest in learning on how programs, such as games, can bring communities together. And eventually, initial innovations in social media platforms such as MySpace and Facebook demonstrated to me the potential that technology had.

Though, contradictory to what this background suggests, I grew up in school highly influeced by the arts. Having spent a majority of my time in school learning and practicing art, I was able to gain fundamental visual arts skills that I now apply to my current work. I treat the front-end of my work as my canvas, always making sure that it is ready for a gallery-style presentation. Due to my enjoyment of technology and the arts, this eventually led me to self-venture into graphic design, photoshop, and video and audio editing where I could practice both of my passions.

I decided to pursue a STEM field in high school and knew that computer science was my field of action because of its great calling for technological advancement and creativity. This decision led me to Georgia Tech where I have been attending since 2015. The rest of my story would be more fun to explain with pictures, therefore, I invite you to learn more about me through my hobbies, volunteer work, and involvement on campus, down below. Thank you.
